The Siberian Husky

Grooming a Siberian Husky

Some basic Information

Their coat's main colors include pure white, black,
sable, agouti, copper, and red. Their coats are a variety
of these colors. These huskies also have what is known
as a double coat, the undercoat and the top coat. The
undercoat is the soft layer of fur closest to the skin. The
purpose of the undercoat is to keep the siberian husky
warm in the winter months. The top coat is made of
long and thick gaurd hairs. The purpose of the undercoat
is to provide protection to the coat and skin, repel water,
block harmful UV rays, hold in the heat of the dog in the
winter months, and allows the skin to breath during the
warm months. Siberian Huskies also have a range of eye
colors as well. They can have two different eye colors or
one solid color of blue, brown, or black.

Some Tips

  • Brush your husky at least once a week
  • When brushing, begin with the undercoat
  • It is recommended to brush with a
    wide-toothed comb then a paddle brush
  • Keep an eye out for shedding
  • They do not need to have their fur cut
  • It is recommended to give them a bath about once a month
