The Siberian Husky

Some History

Some basic Information

As the name suggests, the Siberian Husky is a dog
breed that originates from Siberia in Russia. The
Siberian Husky was not breed for sleding until
about 3,000 years ago by the nomadic Indian tribe
Chukchi. In the 20th century Siberian Huskies were
brought over to North America by fur traders. In the
Siberian Husky was recognized by the American
Kennel Club as the twelth most popular breed. At
the time these dogs started to be used in the U.S.
Army's Arctic Search and Rescue Unit during World
War 2. Today, the Siberian Husky is still used for
sleding by mushers, but is now regarded as a popular
show dog and family pet.

Balto the Heroic Sled Dog

Balto was a Siberian Husky that was a part of a historic
moment in the 1900's that gave siberian huskies a boost
in popularity. In 1925 there was an epidemic of diphtheria
that had broken out in Nome, Alaska. The legendary musher,
Leonard Seppala, and his team of sled dogs, lead by Balto,
traveled 658 miles in only five and a half days. Their trip
was dubbed the "serum run" and they were all named
heroes. Balto died in at the age of 14 in 1933 at a zoo in
Clevland. Currently Balto's remains are in the
Cleveland Museum of Natural History. He also
has a statue in Central Park.
